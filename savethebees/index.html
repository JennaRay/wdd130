Save the Bees

What's happening to our pollinators and how you can help protect them

History

Bee populations have been notibly in decline since the 1990s. Some bee species have already gone extinct, while others are currently endangered (Silva).

According to greenpeace.org, it is usually typical for a bee colony to lose about 5-10% of their population over the winter, 15-20% on a particullarly bad winter. In the US, that rate for bee losses has now reached around 40%, twice as much as it would usually be in a very rough winter. There are even reports of some beekeepers who have a 90% loss rate.3

There is a lot of speculation for what is causing the decline in bee populations. Some are natural causes, like the vicious varraou mites, one of the the worst pests for bees (McChesney)5. Other factors are caused by man, such as habitat loss, use of chemical pesticides and herbicides, and climate change
